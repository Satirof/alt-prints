About

This page was created as a place for students to read final messages from their 2021年度 ALTs; Amani and Luke, as well as learn about some popular holidays overseas.

For the popular holidays, some dates change every year, so these holidays can be found in 'Other'.

Messages


Message from Amani04日03月2021年


Dear Kanagase JHS students,

     First, I would like to say thank you for all your hard work and efforts this past school year. It has been a pleasure to teach English with Mr. Shishido and watching you all enjoy our English lessons. My advice to you all is never give up learning English, always do you best and if there is something you don't know please ask or do some research.

     Second, learning a second language can be difficult. However, it is an opportunity to meet new people and grow in your future jobs. Speaking more than one language is a beautiful gift. It can help you and others.


     Please take care,

     From Ms. Amani.



Message from Luke04日03月2021年


Dear Kanagase JHS students,

     Well done on all of your studies this year, English and other subjects. It has been a lot of fun for me to teach you this past year (and last year too, for you second grade students). I hope you have enjoyed the Wednesday classes when I have come to Kinchu.

     I hope you continue to enjoy studying not only English, but any other language you try. Learning a new language can be very difficult, but once you know the basics it is a great feeling to travel overseas and be able to communicate with native people, even if only a little. So I hope you continue to study and practice any and all languages that you think are interesting.


     Wishing you all the best for your future,

     From Luke Taylor.





Important International Holidays


January


Australia Day

When and Where

January 26

  • Australia

Basic Information

Australia day is a national holiday celebrating the landing of the "First Fleet" at Sydney in 1788.
There are often outdoor concerts, sports competitions and fireworks on this day in Australia.

February


Waitangi Day

When and Where

February 06

  • New Zealand

Basic Information

In New Zealand on February 6, 1840, the 'Treaty of Waitangi' was signed by Maori and British leaders. This day is known as the 'Founding of New Zealand'.

Valentine's Day

When and Where

February 14

  • World Wide

Basic Information

This day is when many lovers celebrate their love for each other. In many western countries; this day is often celebrated by giving gifts to your parter. Such as chocolates, flowers, card, or jewellery.

March


Saint Patrick's Day

When and Where

March 17

  • World Wide

Basic Information

This day is when people celebrate the death of Saint Patrick, a Christian missionary from Ireland.
Many people celebrate by dressing in green clothes, and drinking Irish beer and whiskey.

March Equinox

When and Where

March 21

  • World Wide

Basic Information

This day is when the Earth's tilt and orbit around the sun is perfectly level.
At this time the sun shines directly on the Earth's equator.

April


ANZAC Day

When and Where

April 25

  • Australia
  • New Zealand
  • Cook Islands
  • Niue
  • Samora
  • Tonga

Basic Information

ANZAC (Australia and New Zealand Army Corps) Day is held every year to remember soldiers who fought and died during World War One (1916).
Many people celebrate by attending a memorial service in important locations.
The biggest and most important ceremony is held in Gallipoli (Turkey).


情報リンク（wikipedia）




May


May Day

When and Where

May 1

  • Europe
  • USA
  • Canada

Basic Information

On this holiday people celebrate the first day of summer in many European countries.
Now, America and Canada also celebrate this day.
People celebrate by dancing, singing, and eating cake.
National Worker's Day is also celebrated on this day.


情報リンク（wikipedia）


International Workers' Day

When and Where

May 1

  • World Wide

Basic Information

On this day many workers celebrate the start of "unions" against working companies in the past.
This day is sometimes called "Labour Day" in some countries.
Many countries celebrate by marching in the street, and going to parades.


情報リンク（wikipedia）




June


Summer Solstice

When and Where

June 21

  • Northern Hemisphere

Basic Information

This day is when the Earth's tilt is at maximum towards the sun. As such, on this day the amount of daytime is the longest.
In the southern hemisphere, the summer solstice is on December 21.

October


Halloween

When and Where

October 31

  • World Wide

Basic Information

Halloween is when people celebrate that year's harvest. Many countries celebrate by 'trick or treat'ing (visiting peoples house in cosplay for candy), going to costume parties, or visiting a 'haunted house'.

November


Guy Fawkes Night/Bonfire Night

When and Where

November 5

  • Great Britain
  • Commonwealth Nations

Basic Information

In 1605 a man named 'Guy Fawkes' and his friends tried to destroy important building to change England's leader. This plan failed, so the day is celebrated for the failure.
Many people make 'bonfires' or use fireworks to celebrate on this night.

Other


Chinese New Year

When and Where

Between January and February

  • China
  • World Wide

Basic Information

On this day China celebrates the New Year. The date is chosen from lunar and solar calendars. Celebrations start from two-weeks earlier. Many countries in the world also celebrate the Chinese New Year.
It is also called the 'Spring Festival' and 'Lunar New Year'.


情報リンク（wikipedia）


Easter

When and Where

Changes every year

  • World Wide

Basic Information

Easter is a Christian holiday remembering the death and resurrection (回生) of Jesus Christ. But, many non-Christians also celebrate Easter by the giving and receiving of chocolates and sweets. Often a chocolate rabbit or egg is used for non-Christian easter celebrations.
